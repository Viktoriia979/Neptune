Neptune

Neptune is the eighth and farthest planet from the Sun and is the fourth-largest planet in the Solar System. It is an ice giant made mostly of hydrogen, helium, and icy materials such as water, ammonia, and methane. Neptune has a deep blue color caused by methane in its atmosphere and is the windiest planet in the Solar System, with winds reaching speeds of over 2,000 kilometers per hour (1,200 miles per hour).

Neptune rotates on its axis in about 16 hours and takes about 165 Earth years to complete one orbit around the Sun. The planet has a faint ring system and 16 known moons, including Triton, its largest moon. Neptune's atmosphere contains huge storms, including dark spots that are similar to Jupiter's Great Red Spot.

Neptune is named after the Roman god of the sea because of its deep blue appearance. Since it is so far from the Sun, Neptune is extremely cold and difficult to study. Scientists use spacecraft and telescopes to learn more about Neptune, its moons, and the outer regions of the Solar System.
